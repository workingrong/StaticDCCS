Our Courses

Great Courses

Most of our classes are in-person classes, provided at Garnet Valley Middle School campus on Sundays, to serve students in the areas neighboring Garnet Valley, including North Wilmington, Glen Mills, Chadds Ford, Media, West Chester, etc. Our in-person classes are designed in such a way that students can learn in a fun and interactive manner.

Some of our classes are taught online. Founded in 2020, when the pandemic significantly disrupted school education and impaired socialization, our online courses and activities during the 2020-2021 school year greatly benefited students. In the future, we will carry on this tradition and provide some classes virtually based on demand.

We also have classes for adults, such as dance, art, and sports. For parents interested in taking any of the lessons, we believe it's a great way to learn and foster a sense of community building.

Chinese

We offer both Chinese as Heritage Language (CHL) classes and Chinese as Second Language (CSL) classes. For CHL classes, Ma Liping Chinese (马力平中文), Zhengquan Chinese（整全中文）and Meizhou Huayu (美洲华语) textbooks are used. For CSL classes, we have used Go! Chinese textbooks in the past, but we are open to other options too, should parents have other preferences.

Coding

We cooperate with Delaware STEAM Academy and provide coding courses from Scratch, Python, Javascript to Raspberry Pi for K-12 students with different programming skills. Programming languages are taught based on projects and the fundamentals and theory are introduced and blended in each project to support students’ problem-solving abilities and build the projects properly and correctly.

Lego Robotics

Built around the Lego Mindstorms robot set and the Scratch language, our Lego Robotics class breaks down STEM concepts into hands-on lessons to explore robotics, mechanical systems, electronics, and programming. Students learn how coding skills are used in robotics and how to incorporate these skills into their own robots.

Public Speaking

Public speaking classes are taught by Mike Nie, founder of “Speak to lead LLC”. Coach Nie has won many awards in public speaking and is a distinguished toastmaster. He has years of experience teaching public speaking. In each meeting, speaking techniques are taught through watching and analyzing famous speeches. After each lesson, students are assigned a 2-3 min speech homework to be presented during class. Homework submission is analyzed individually by the coach both offline and during class.
